Most interior designers rely on referrals and Instagram. When referrals dry up, revenue drops. They don't have time to prospect, write cold emails, follow up, or build a pipeline while also running projects. They need someone to do it for them.
85% of designers rely on word-of-mouth alone
The Solution

A Done-For-You Outreach Machine

Whitmore Groups identifies ideal clients for each designer (property developers, homeowners, hospitality groups, etc.), builds targeted lists, and runs personalised outreach campaigns — emails, DMs, LinkedIn — until bookings land in the designer's calendar.
Full pipeline ownership. Designer focuses on delivery only.

Stress Test

Don't protect my feelings. Protect my future. Does this survive reality?

5-Point Reality Check — Whitmore Groups Interiors Plan

1

Devil's Advocate: Interior designers are notoriously hard to sell to. Many are one-person studios with tiny budgets and no trust in marketing agencies. You'll face "I can't afford this" constantly — even from people who need you.

⚡ Counter: Target designers with an existing portfolio and 3+ years in business. They've felt the referral drought. Lead with a performance guarantee — "X booked calls or you don't pay for month 2."
2

Weak Links: Your results depend on cold email deliverability, which Google and Outlook actively destroy. One spam complaint can kill a domain. If deliverability breaks, your client results tank — and so does retention.

⚡ Counter: Always warm up domains for 3 weeks minimum. Run 2–3 sending domains per client. Monitor bounce rates weekly. Use Smartlead's inbox rotation feature.
3

Uncomfortable Question: Can you actually guarantee results? If you promise 10 booked calls and deliver 3, the client churns and leaves a bad review. Overclaiming to close sales will destroy the business faster than anything else.

⚡ Counter: Sell "qualified replies" not "booked calls." Replies you can control. Bookings depend on the designer's sales skills. Frame your guarantee around what you own.
4

Real-World Comparison: There are already well-funded lead gen agencies targeting creative professionals. Agencies like Magnetic and Leads for Creatives have brand recognition, testimonials, and volume pricing. You're entering a crowded space with zero track record.

⚡ Counter: Hyper-niche is your moat. Don't say "lead gen agency." Say "the only outreach agency built specifically for interior designers." Niche positioning beats brand recognition at the $2K price point.
5

Suggested Upgrade: The current model is pure service. Time-for-money. To scale past $25K/month without burning out, you need a productised offer, templated delivery, and eventually a software or data layer that competitors can't easily copy.

⚡ Long-term: Build a proprietary lead database of UK property developers and hospitality buyers segmented by active project signals. License it. That's a defensible asset — not just another agency.

Cold Call Script

Built for calling luxury builders and developers on behalf of interior designers. Every section is a module — if they interrupt, you know exactly where to pick back up.

HOW TO USE THIS SCRIPT
✦ Read it out loud 10 times before your first call. It should feel like your own words, not a script.
✦ Never rush. Luxury developers respect calm confidence. Slow down by 20% from your natural speed.
✦ Silence is your friend. After asking a question — stop talking. Let them fill the gap.
✦ You are not begging. You are offering them access to a designer who elevates their projects. Come from that frame.
Module 1 — The Opener (First 15 seconds)
Do NOT skip or shorten this
YOU SAY
"Hi, is this [Developer's Name]?

Good [morning/afternoon] — my name is [Your Name], I'm calling from Whitmore Groups.

I'll be straight with you — this is a cold call, and I have something specific I wanted to run by you. Do you have 60 seconds?"
Why this works: Saying "this is a cold call" disarms them immediately. Most developers respect the honesty and say yes to 60 seconds. Asking permission puts them in control — they're more likely to listen.
Module 2 — The Hook (The one sentence that keeps them on the line)
Most critical part
YOU SAY
"We work with a highly regarded interior designer who specialises in luxury residential and commercial projects — the kind that adds real value to a developer's portfolio.

They're looking to take on [1–2] more projects this quarter, and based on your work in [area/project type], I thought there could be a strong fit."
Why this works: You're not selling a service — you're offering access to a scarce resource. "Taking on 1–2 more projects" signals the designer is in demand, not desperate. Mentioning their specific area shows you did homework.
Module 3 — The Bridge (What's in it for them)
Keep this under 30 seconds
YOU SAY
"What they bring to a project is a finished space that photographs well, sells faster, and justifies a premium price point.

Developers they've worked with have seen [faster sales / stronger valuations / better buyer response] — especially on luxury units where presentation is everything.

I'm not asking you to commit to anything today. I just want to know — is interior design something you currently factor into your projects?"
Why this works: You're speaking their language — ROI, speed of sale, price per sq ft. The closing question is soft and diagnostic, not pushy. It opens a conversation rather than forcing a yes or no on the pitch.
Module 4 — Objection Handlers
🚫 "We already have an interior designer."
"That's great — most serious developers do. I'm not here to replace anyone. I'm just curious — are you happy with the turnaround time and the finish quality on your current projects, or is there room for improvement?"
→ Gets them talking. If they say "yes happy" — ask if they'd be open to a backup for overflow. If they say "there's room" — you're in.
🚫 "We don't have budget for this right now."
"Completely understand. Just to give you context — there's no upfront cost on your side. Our designer works on a project basis, so it only comes into play when you have a project that needs it. Would it be worth just having a quick conversation so you know the option exists?"
→ Reframes cost as zero-risk. Plants a seed for future projects.
🚫 "Send me an email / WhatsApp."
"Of course — I'll send that across right now. Before I do, just so I can tailor what I send — what type of projects are you working on at the moment? Residential, commercial, or a mix?"
→ Never just say "okay bye." Keep them talking for 30 more seconds. The info you gather makes your follow-up 10× stronger.
🚫 "I'm busy, call me later."
"Absolutely — I respect your time. Can I confirm a specific time that works? I want to make sure I'm not calling at the wrong moment again."
→ Gets a committed callback time instead of a vague "later." Always repeat the time back to confirm.
🚫 "How did you get my number?"
"Your details are publicly listed — I came across your projects online and thought the work was impressive enough to reach out directly. I wouldn't be calling if I didn't think there was a genuine fit here."
→ Calm, honest, confident. Turns the challenge into a compliment.
Module 5 — The Close (Book the meeting, not the deal)
Goal = 1 meeting booked
YOU SAY
"I'd love to set up a quick 15-minute call between you and the designer — no commitment, just a conversation to see if the timing works for your next project.

Are you free sometime [this week / early next week] — even just 15 minutes?"
The golden rule of the close: You are not closing a deal on this call. You are closing a meeting. One step at a time. A booked 15-minute call is a win. Treat it like one.
